CLAIMS
[0029] [0029] 1. A computer-implemented method for privacy-preserving verification of health data using zero-knowledge cryptographic proofs, the method comprising: receiving health data from one or more health monitoring devices; defining constraint parameters for health data verification, wherein the constraint parameters include predetermined physiological ranges and temporal requirements; generating a zero-knowledge proof that demonstrates satisfaction of the constraint parameters without revealing underlying health data values; transmitting the zero-knowledge proof to a verification entity; and receiving confirmation of health data verification based on the zero-knowledge proof validation.

[0030] 2. The method of claim 1, wherein the health data includes at least one of: heart rate measurements, blood pressure readings, activity levels, sleep patterns, or metabolic indicators.

[0031] 3. The method of claim 1, wherein generating the zero-knowledge proof comprises: creating constraint satisfaction circuits that encode biological plausibility rules; applying temporal validation logic to ensure data consistency; and computing cryptographic proofs using zk-SNARKs, zk-STARKs, or Bulletproofs protocols.

[0032] 4. The method of claim 1, further comprising: aggregating health data from multiple sources; resolving conflicts between different data sources; and computing reliability scores for each data source.

[0033] 5. The method of claim 1, wherein the constraint parameters are dynamically adjusted based on user demographics, medical history, or regulatory requirements.

[0034] 6. The method of claim 1, further comprising: generating audit trails for all verification activities; capturing user consent for data processing; and implementing data deletion mechanisms in compliance with privacy regulations.

[0035] 7. The method of claim 1, wherein the zero-knowledge proof is anchored to a blockchain network for immutable storage and third-party verification.

[0036] 8. A zero-knowledge health verification system comprising: a processor; a memory coupled to the processor; a health data interface configured to receive biometric data from external health monitoring devices; a constraint validation module configured to evaluate health data against predetermined parameters; a zero-knowledge proof generator configured to create cryptographic proofs without revealing sensitive health information; and a verification interface configured to transmit proofs to external verification entities.

[0037] 9. The system of claim 8, wherein the constraint validation module is configured to perform: activity metric validation; sleep quality validation; and metabolic measurement validation.

[0038] 10. The system of claim 8, further comprising: a user consent manager; an audit trail generator; and a regulatory compliance module for GDPR and HIPAA reporting.

[0039] 11. A non-transitory computer-readable storage medium storing instructions that, when executed by a processor, cause the processor to: securely connect to health data sources; normalize and validate biometric data; generate a zero-knowledge proof of compliance with biometric constraints; transmit the proof to a verification entity; and maintain cryptographic privacy throughout the process.

[0040] 12. The storage medium of claim 11, wherein the instructions further cause the processor to: generate audit trails and consent metadata; apply adaptive constraint parameters based on user profile data; and support integration with EMR/EHR systems while preserving data privacy.

[0041] 13. The system of claim 8, wherein the zero-knowledge proof generator supports at least one of: zk-SNARKs, zk-STARKs, Bulletproofs, or quantum-resistant cryptographic protocols.

[0042] 14. The method of claim 1, wherein the zero-knowledge proof is transmitted using a secure API compliant with REST or GraphQL specifications.

[0043] 15. The method of claim 1, further comprising evaluating biometric data from multiple devices, resolving conflicts, and computing reliability scores for each source.

[0044] 16. The system of claim 8, wherein the health data interface is configured to communicate with wearable sensors, mobile health apps, or remote diagnostic platforms.

[0045] 17. The system of claim 8, wherein the constraint validation module supports integration of third-party constraints defined by regulators, insurers, or researchers.

[0046] 18. The method of claim 1, further comprising generating different levels of proof complexity based on verification context, including basic, intermediate, and regulatory-grade validation tiers.

[0047] 19. The system of claim 8, wherein the verification interface supports on-demand validation, scheduled audits, and conditional triggers based on data thresholds.

[0048] 20. The storage medium of claim 11, further comprising documentation and APIs for integration into external healthcare or research systems.

[0049] 21. The system of claim 8, wherein the zero-knowledge proof generator is configured to utilize GPU or ASIC acceleration for constraint satisfaction solving.

[0050] 22. The method of claim 1, wherein the verification entity interfaces with both a blockchain ledger and an enterprise health record system.

[0051] 23. The method of claim 1, wherein the zero-knowledge proof is generated using a multi-party computation protocol such that no single party possesses the full biometric dataset.

[0052] 24. The system of claim 8, wherein the constraint validation module is further configured to dynamically adjust constraint parameters based on user-specific data including biometric trends, demographic profiles, geographic context, and real-time environmental factors, enabling personalized validation while maintaining zero-knowledge privacy guarantees.
